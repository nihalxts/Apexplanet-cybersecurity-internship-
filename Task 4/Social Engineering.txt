1. Definition and Concept

Social engineering is a method of manipulating people to reveal confidential information. It focuses on human behavior rather than technical vulnerabilities.

2. Phishing Attacks

Phishing involves creating fake websites or emails that look real. Users unknowingly enter their login credentials, which are then stolen by attackers.

3. Pretexting and Baiting

Pretexting involves pretending to be a trusted person to gain information. Baiting uses attractive offers (like free downloads) to trick users into compromising security.

4. Human Weakness

Social engineering works because it exploits trust, fear, and curiosity. Even secure systems can be compromised if users are not careful.

5. Prevention

Awareness and training are the best defenses. Users should verify sources before sharing information and avoid clicking suspicious links.
